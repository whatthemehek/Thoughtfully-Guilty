According to the SEIA, "Community solar refers to local solar facilities shared by multiple community subscribers who receive credit on their electricity bills for their share of the power produced." Programs such as these bring the benefits of rooftop solar, such as offsetting the costs of energy bills and a greener source of energy, to people who may not have the capability to build a rooftop solar grid. With community solar, depending on the share of the community grid that you purchase, you will receive a credit on your each month equivalent to your share's percent of the total energy generated by the grid that month.
